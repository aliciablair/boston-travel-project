Why Boston?

The Athens of America

Boston is the capital and largest city of the Commonwealth of Massachusetts and the cultural and financial center of the New England region of the Northeastern United States.

Boston is a symbol of much that has gone into the development of the American consciousness, and its presence reaches far beyond its immediate environs. As the spiritual capital of the New England states and as the earliest centre of American culture, Boston has influenced the country for some three centuries. Boston has remained the focal point of what may be the most diversified and dynamic combination of educational, cultural, medical, and scientific activities in the United States.

My favorite destinations in Boston

Freedom Trail Tour

This tour highlights the diverse history that took place at 11 of the 16 official Freedom Trail historic sites. Experience stories of the American Revolution, as well as the history of traditionally underrepresented peoples spanning from the 16th to 21st centuries.

Address:

139 Tremont Street, Boston, MA 02111

What I like about it

This is one of the best introductions to the history of Boston, features historic buildings, and is a great way to walk around Boston and submerse yourself back in time.

Life Alive Organic Café

Life Alive is here to provide satisfying, energizing and delightful nourishment in a cozy and inviting atmosphere. In this Urban Oasis, one might see a colorful spectrum of dishes, art and people, and, most importantly, taste craveable fresh flavors that fuel the body.

Address:

431 Boylston St, Boston, MA 02116

What I like about it

This is one of the best spots in Boston to fuel your body with whole foods and and reenergize in an uplifting atmosphere. I love this café as a vegan individual.

Gardner Museum

The Isabella Stewart Gardner Museum is an art museum in which houses significant examples of European, Asian, and American art. Its collection includes paintings, sculpture, tapestries, and decorative arts. It is home to the biggest unsolved art heist in history.

Address:

25 Evans Way Boston, MA 02115

What I like about it

This museum is unlike any other. It is absolutely stunning and covered with beautiful pieces. I love spending a day here and it feels like a european dream of art and mystery.
